## Challenger — Risks & Edge Cases

- **Finding 2 contradicts a standing decision.** decisions.md 2026-07-09 (UR-012) dropped an equivalent load()-overlay finding as *unreachable* — "CI restores committed timings.json only, no pending present and WARP_TIMINGS off at shard time." Finding 2's failure scenario requires pending batches present at load() time. Capture must either re-litigate that decision (the local/dev `warp shard` path can see pending) or scope finding 2 down to a warn-instead-of-silent-reset fix; shipping it as a "divergent CI plans" bug contradicts recorded reasoning. Triggered by finding 2.
- **The completeness redesign supersedes UR-013's recorded semantics — and workers treat decisions.md as constraints.** decisions.md 2026-07-09 (UR-013) fixed three completeness semantics including the in-flight-test shutdown discriminator; findings 4/5/14/15/16 demonstrate those semantics are structurally broken. run-worker reads decisions.md as standing constraints, so if capture does not append a superseding decision line, workers implementing the redesign will be pulling against the old constraint. A superseding entry must be part of the capture output. Triggered by the "Design guidance" section.
- **The one-design-fix guidance conflicts with the UR-015 precedent.** decisions.md 2026-07-09 (UR-015) recorded "narrow bug-fix REQs, one per confirmed runtime failure path; independent regression paths are clearer than subsystem mega-REQs." The accepted guidance for UR-016 is the opposite for the completeness cluster. This is a deliberate exception and should be recorded as such, not silently diverged. Triggered by the "Design guidance" section.
- Findings 1 and 9 (path-form keying, lazy relative dir) are best fixed by recording root metadata in the timings artifact and absolutizing at construction — a schema change. Clean-break schema changes are pre-authorized (UR-011 decision, zero public users), but the change ripples into every merge-machinery finding (3, 7, 18) touching the same ~150 lines of TimingStore. If the schema REQ doesn't land first, every later TimingStore REQ rebases against churn. Order: schema/root fix → merge hardening → completeness redesign → cleanups. Triggered by findings 1, 3, 7, 9, 18.
- Finding 8 (.gitignore entry uncommitted) is working-tree state, not code — it will silently vanish if no REQ claims it, because workers operate in worktrees branched from HEAD (which lacks the entry). Needs an explicit tiny REQ or folding into the first committed REQ. Triggered by finding 8.

## Connector — Links & Reuse

- Findings 11, 12, 13, 19 are all "hand-rolled discovery diverges from PHPUnit's" — symlinks, config filename probing, suffixes, hidden dirs. `sebastian/file-iterator` (PHPUnit's own walker) is already installed as a transitive dependency; replacing TestFileFinder's RecursiveDirectoryIterator with it, and extending SuiteDiscovery's probe list to PHPUnit's exact filename order, fixes all four by construction and keeps parity automatically as PHPUnit evolves. One substitution REQ beats four patch REQs. Triggered by findings 11, 12, 13, 19.
- Findings 6, 7, 20 share one root: the CLI exception policy is a hand-copied `catch (InvalidArgumentException|RuntimeException)` in four places that misses JsonException and ValueError, and readMerged's throw-vs-degrade choice is inconsistent with its siblings. Hoisting one `catch (Throwable)` boundary into WarpCli::run (already flagged as a confirmed cleanup in round 1) plus a degrade-with-warning in readMerged addresses all three and deletes the duplication. Triggered by findings 6, 7, 20.
- Finding 10 (Stderr bypasses injected streams) is the same defect as round 1's confirmed cleanup finding; fixing it means threading a stream/callback into TimingStore — which touches the same constructors the finding-1 schema fix touches. Same REQ chain, adjacent commits. Triggered by finding 10.
- Findings 9 and 17 are direct extensions of prior hardening REQs (REQ-089 cwd fallback, REQ-082 nonfatal flush) whose fixes were narrower than the bug class. REQ bodies should cite those archive files so workers see the prior intent and don't re-shrink the fix. Triggered by findings 9, 17.

## Summary

Three things must be settled before capture: (1) finding 2 collides with UR-012's recorded "unreachable" decision and needs re-litigation or rescoping; (2) the completeness redesign both supersedes UR-013's recorded semantics and consciously breaks UR-015's narrow-REQ precedent — both need superseding decisions.md entries, and the redesign needs a concrete design (file-scoped completeness under paratest slicing) before it can decompose into verifiable REQs; (3) four discovery findings collapse into one "adopt PHPUnit's own file iterator" REQ and three CLI-robustness findings collapse into one exception-policy REQ, so the natural decomposition is roughly 10–12 REQs, not 20, ordered schema-fix-first to avoid TimingStore rebase churn.
